About Me

e-Commerce from screen to package at the door.

I design and maintain the systems that connect digital experience, operational reality, and customer delivery—so e-commerce works all the way through.

Where this started

Early in my career, I stepped into a situation where website traffic dropped by 30% overnight due to a failed platform migration. Thus began my journey in e-commerce and IT, where I learned the importance of 301 redirects, SEO, and the importance of a well-structured website.

Recovery wasn’t instant, but it was methodical. Beyond fixing technical issues, attention to user experience, customer journey, and the supporting systems led to steady gains that eventually exceeded previous highs.

A career shaped by systems

I started as a webmaster, became a software developer, and eventually moved into IT and e-commerce leadership.

Across every role, one thing stayed constant: an understanding of how technical constraints, data quality, and design decisions compound over time.

I’ve seen how yesterday’s shortcuts become today’s bottlenecks— and how thoughtful systems design can reverse that trajectory.

Most problems aren’t technical

Technology is rarely the limiting factor. What usually breaks down is how systems operate together.

  • How data is structured (or not)
  • How platforms integrate
  • How decisions get encoded into software
  • How users experience the result

My work focuses on restoring coherence across these layers so organizations can move forward without fighting their own infrastructure.

Infinite Frameworks

The focus is practical and outcome-driven: fixing what’s broken, simplifying what’s bloated, and building systems that support growth instead of resisting it.

What I don’t do

I avoid over-engineering and unnecessary abstraction.

I believe deeply in KISS—not as a shortcut, but as a discipline. The best systems are the ones people can understand, trust, and maintain.

My edge

I bring deep technical knowledge across the full stack, paired with strategic and operational awareness.

That allows me to see issues others miss—especially at the seams between business intent, technical reality, and user experience.

How I define success

I know I’ve done my job when the solution meets the business need, reduces friction, and holds up over time.

If systems are quieter, teams are faster, and decisions are clearer—the work worked.
