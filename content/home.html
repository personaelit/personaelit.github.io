The problem I’m usually solving

Over time, organizations accumulate technical and design decisions that once made sense—but no longer do. These choices live on as fragmented data, inconsistent interfaces, and brittle workflows.

The result is friction everywhere: for customers, for internal teams, and for leadership trying to make informed decisions.

External systems — customer & conversions

These are the systems customers touch. They shape perception, trust, and conversion.

  • User experience & journey design
    Reducing friction across discovery, configuration, and checkout.
  • Content & merchandising systems
    Structuring content so it scales without losing clarity.
  • Analytics & performance insight
    Turning traffic and engagement data into actionable signal.

The goal is not novelty—it’s confidence, speed, and clarity for the customer.

Internal systems — operations & leverage

These systems are rarely visible to customers, but they determine how fast—and how safely—an organization can move.

  • Data integrity & integration
    Cleaning, connecting, and governing data across platforms.
  • Internal tooling & automation
    Reducing manual work, errors, and operational drag.
  • Process-aware software
    Tools that reflect how teams actually work, not how diagrams say they should.

These systems create leverage—saving time, reducing errors, and enabling better decisions.

The bridge — why both matter

Many organizations optimize these systems in isolation. Customer experience teams work around broken data. Operations teams optimize processes customers never feel.

My value lives in the overlap. I design external systems that are supported by internal reality, and internal systems that serve real customer outcomes.
