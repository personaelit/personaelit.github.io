# Violence & Paranoia

Behind Duke’s bravado lurks fear: of arrest, of biker gangs, of ‘bad vibes.’ Guns and nightsticks appear as props in the Vegas cabaret, yet the threat feels real because society’s rules have blurred (see [[Moral Ambiguity & Law]]). Heightened paranoia is a by‑product of relentless [[Drug Use|Counterculture & Drug Use]] and the cacophony of casino spectacle, completing the novel’s cycle of dread.
