# American Dream

Hunter S. Thompson frames Las Vegas—the ersatz capital of **instant wealth and neon‑lit promise**—as a carnival mirror of the American Dream. Raoul Duke and Dr. Gonzo roam through casinos that promise prosperity yet drain their bankrolls, underscoring how the Dream has shifted from self‑determination to **empty consumption**. The quest becomes a hallucinated road‑trip pilgrimage, linking directly to [[Death of the American Dream]] and illuminating how unchecked [[Consumerism & Excess]] supplants authentic aspiration.
