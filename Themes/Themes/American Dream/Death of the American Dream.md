# Death of the American Dream

By 1971 the utopian hope of the 1960s lies in the gutter of the Strip. Thompson’s narration treats Las Vegas as a mausoleum where ideals are embalmed beneath corporate slogans and sportsbook odds. Duke’s drug‑soaked detachment is less rebellion than resignation: the system is rigged, leaving only frantic self‑medication. This fatalism intertwines with [[Moral Ambiguity & Law]], while the corpse of the Dream is pumped full of amphetamines by [[Counterculture & Drug Use]].
