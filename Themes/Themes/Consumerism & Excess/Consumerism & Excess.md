# Consumerism & Excess

From $50 room‑service tabs to rented Cadillacs, conspicuous consumption is both sport and satire. Duke’s spending spree dramatizes the nation’s shift from production to consumption, anchoring *Fear and Loathing*’s scathing commentary on rampant materialism. This appetite fattens the corpse of the [[American Dream]] while feeding the spectacle analyzed in [[Media and Spectacle]].
